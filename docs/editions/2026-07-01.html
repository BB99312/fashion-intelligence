Purple Reigns as Resale Sprints Toward $393 Billion

Chanel's Blazy era and a regal-purple wave define the season, while Swatch x AP mania, luxury CEO churn and a booming secondhand market reshape what shoppers actually buy.

Executive Summary
  • Purple is the throughline — the shade tops summer street style and is set to dominate fall/winter runways, from Chanel to Celine [Source].
  • Blazy's Chanel changed the map — Matthieu Blazy's SS26 debut helped push Chanel to the top of Lyst's Q1 2026 hottest-brands index [Source].
  • Swatch x AP led the hype — the "Royal Pop" topped Complex's most-hyped list of 2026 so far, ahead of Supreme x MM6 and NIGO's Nike AF1 [Source].
  • Resale is taking share — ThredUp projects the global secondhand market at $393B by 2030, with 2026 U.S. online resale hitting $31B (directional) [Source].
  • Luxury leadership churned — Versace's CEO exited for Prada, Rabanne lost Julien Dossena, and Alexander McQueen named ex-Prada boss Gianfranco D'Attis CEO [Source].
  • NYFW Spring 2027 is set — the CFDA calendar runs Sept. 10–15 with 70 shows and newcomers including Conner Ives [Source].
01 Current Season Trends Summer 2026, on the street and in stores

The summer palette skews bright and saturated. Fashion editors report a spread running from rich purple and tomato red to viral chartreuse, with pale blue everywhere [Source].

Colors
Purple, tomato red, lime chartreuse, turquoise, light pink, pale blue, citrus yellow and azure blue lead the summer set — citrus tones are emerging as a bolder alternative to last year's butter-yellow [Source].
Silhouettes
The "CBK effect" keeps column skirts and crisp white button-downs in rotation, while oversized graphic tees are being belted as beach cover-ups or paired with skirt suits [Source].
Fabrics
A "sexy summer" pushes leather worn in the heat, offset with sheer, airy lace and luminous satin — led by Hermès, Tom Ford and Gucci [Source].
Patterns
Graphic tees and World Cup jerseys are the print story of the moment, styled with vintage Levi's, poplin skirts and ballet flats [Source].

The collection shaping it all is Chanel under Matthieu Blazy: his spring/summer 2026 debut offered a playful, modern twist on the house's codes and helped Chanel top Lyst's Q1 2026 hottest-brands index [Source].

02 Next Season Predictions What the Fall/Winter 2026 runways signaled

If summer flirts with purple, fall commits to it. Editors describe a "regal-purple reign" that grew from a spring murmur into a roar, with practically every FW26 show featuring the shade [Source].

Colors
Vibrant regal purple dominates the season; at Celine, Michael Rider accented a purple leather look, part of a near-universal runway embrace [Source].
Silhouettes
Proportions swing back to "shrink wrap" — very fitted, with Michael Rider's skinny flared pants the talk of Paris — plus a doubling-down on skirt suits from Tom Ford and The Row [Source].
Fabrics
"Modern heirlooms" bring rich brocade, jacquard, embroidery and beading at Dior, Valentino and Conner Ives, while patchwork shearling adds a feral edge [Source].
Patterns
"Le Smoking" tuxedo dressing ripples across shows — bow ties at Khaite and McQueen, satin and cummerbunds at Michael Kors and Altuzarra — alongside checked suiting [Source].

The mood-setter was Prada, whose "Window Dressing" FW26 show sent just 15 models out four times each in subtly tweaked looks — a meditation on multiplicity that framed the season's restraint [Source].

04 Consumer Behavior Where the money is actually going

Value and circularity keep pulling spend. ThredUp's 14th annual report finds U.S. secondhand apparel grew 19% in 2025 — its strongest year since 2021 and 3.6x the broader clothing market [Source]. All market figures below are directional.

The report projects global secondhand reaching $393B by 2030 at roughly 9% annual growth, about 10% of all apparel spend, with 59% of consumers buying secondhand in 2025 [Source]. Mass and mid-tier brands drive volume: J.Crew and Zara head the most-shopped resale brands, followed by Ann Taylor Loft, Old Navy and Gap [Source].

AI is entering the closet, too: 69% of surveyed consumers said they'd let AI agents monitor resale platforms 24/7 to hunt "holy grail" items, and 63% would let an agent negotiate deals [Source].

+ Industry & City Pulse Boardroom moves and what's happening on the ground

June closed with heavy luxury leadership churn. Versace CEO Emmanuel Gintzburger is leaving for Prada; Alexander McQueen named former Prada chief Gianfranco D'Attis CEO effective June 3; and Rabanne confirmed Julien Dossena's exit after 13 years as creative director [Source].

Balenciaga appointed ex-Nike and Vans exec Drieke Leenknegt as CMO, Saint Laurent named Anouck Duranteau-Loeper deputy CEO effective today, July 1, and Carven poached Kai Nesselrath from Saint Laurent as design director [Source].

New York
The CFDA unveiled its NYFW Spring 2027 schedule — Sept. 10–15, 70 shows and presentations — with newcomers Conner Ives, Magda Butrym and Zaldy joining familiar names [Source].
Los Angeles
Editors returning from a month in L.A. flagged knee-length skirts, relaxed jeans, non-black ballet flats and oversized button-downs as the summer uniform [Source].
Chicago
Hometown storyteller Joe Freshgoods kept Chicago on the streetwear map with his '90s-inspired "Let's Get Weird" New Balance drop [Source].
