Loud Luxury, Resale Natives and a World Cup Wardrobe

Saturated color and tactile texture rule the runways as couture week looms, while secondhand keeps outrunning retail and the World Cup turns streetwear into a global uniform.

Executive Summary
  • Color is the message. Cobalt, chartreuse, burnt orange and Tiffany-toned turquoise dominate summer, a deliberate rejection of quiet-luxury neutrals [Source].
  • Fall pivots to texture. The FW26 runways pushed fur, shearling, fringe and sweeping trains, led by Louise Trotter's Bottega Veneta and Matthieu Blazy's Chanel [Source].
  • Couture week is next. Paris Haute Couture runs July 6–9 with debuts from Pierpaolo Piccioli at Balenciaga, Duran Lantink at Jean Paul Gaultier and India's Manish Malhotra [Source].
  • Resale keeps compounding. Secondhand grew 13% last year and is forecast to hit $393B by 2030 — twice the pace of apparel overall (directional) [Source].
  • The World Cup is fashion's biggest brief. Brands from Palace to Jacquemus, NOCTA and Willy Chavarria built capsules around the 2026 tournament [Source].
  • Money moves. Reformation filed for an IPO on $507M in sales as Chanel topped the Lyst Index under Blazy [Source][Source].
01 Current Season Trends Summer 2026 leans loud, saturated and texturally rich

The defining feature of summer is the near-total absence of muted neutrals — a firm rejection of the quiet-luxury aesthetic that dominated recent years, with editors framing it as "loud" luxury [Source]. The collections doing the shaping: Matthieu Blazy's frenzy-inducing Chanel debut, plus Prada, Burberry and Celine, all of which leaned into vivid, expressive color [Source].

Colors
Turquoise/Tiffany blue is the season's signal shade — a toothpaste tone at Chanel, classic turquoise at Burberry and Prada — alongside cobalt, chartreuse, burnt orange and regal purple [Source][Source].
Silhouettes
Sporty references run through the season — reworked jerseys at Area, racerback leather tanks at Balenciaga — plus the breezy balloon pant and a wave of mini dresses [Source].
Fabrics
Silk scarves are doubling as tops and belts (Chanel Cruise, Hermès, Kallmeyer), while crisp poplin and all-white linen dressing channel a Carolyn Bessette Kennedy revival [Source].
Patterns
Prints are louder than they've been since the '70s and '80s — retro florals at Chloé, '80s graphics at Louis Vuitton [Source].
02 Next Season Predictions FW26's texture story, couture debuts and a quieter color reset

The Fall/Winter 2026 runways — the sophomore outings for fashion's new guard — point the way forward. Demna's Gucci divided opinion, Blazy's Chanel "ignited joy," Jonathan Anderson was Dior's golden boy and Pieter Mulier took his final bow at Alaïa [Source]. Next on the calendar: Paris Haute Couture, July 6–9, following Cruise 2027 spectacles that put Gucci in Times Square, Dior in L.A. and Louis Vuitton inside the Frick Collection [Source].

Colors
WGSN/Coloro's FW26/27 palette signals transition: earthy Cocoa Powder and soft Wax Paper grounded by accents of Fresh Purple, Green Glow and Transformative Teal — with Pantone's "Cloud Dancer" white as the calm base [Source].
Silhouettes
The "Long Exit": trains everywhere, from Dior's frothy tutu skirts to organza tails at McQueen and bias-cut silk trailing from the hips at Kallmeyer [Source].
Fabrics
Tactile texture is the headline — fur, pile, shearling and fringe overflowed at Louise Trotter's Bottega Veneta, with scale-like layered sequins at Chanel and shearling across Louis Vuitton [Source].
Patterns
Statement floral appliqués and rosette ribbons replaced jewelry pins — oversized blooms at Dior and Chanel, rosettes at Simone Rocha, flower pins on stark black jackets at Khaite [Source].

Couture week brings the marquee debuts: Pierpaolo Piccioli's first couture for Balenciaga, Duran Lantink's debut couture for Jean Paul Gaultier, and Indian designer Manish Malhotra making his Paris Haute Couture bow on July 8 [Source].

04 Consumer Behavior Resale natives, value hunting and supply-side strain

Secondhand is no longer the second option. ThredUp's 2026 report (figures directional, via GlobalData) found the global secondhand apparel market grew 13% last year while new-apparel sales were nearly flat [Source]. CEO James Reinhart points to "resale natives" — shoppers who grew up buying and selling their wardrobes — as the durable engine, while supply, not demand, is now the sector's primary constraint, and nearly half of discovery happens off-platform across social feeds [Source].

05 Streetwear A World Cup gold rush and a Vans-led skate revival

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the single biggest force in streetwear right now, with capsules from Willy Chavarria, KidSuper, Cactus Jack, Kith and more [Source]. The standout was Nike's "X2" program pairing seven federations with seven collaborators: Canada × NOCTA, England × Palace, France × Jacquemus, Netherlands × Patta, Nigeria × Slawn, South Korea × PEACEMINUSONE and USA × V.A.A. + Industry & City Pulse Boardroom moves and what's happening on the ground

Breaking moves: Chanel took the No. 1 spot on the Lyst Index for Q1 2026 under Matthieu Blazy, ahead of Saint Laurent and Dior [Source]. On the talent front, Meryll Rogge was named creative director of Marni, succeeding Francesco Risso [Source], while Carven appointed Kai Nesselrath as design director in June, hired from Saint Laurent [Source]. In retail, Reformation filed for a U.S. IPO, and Saks Global re-emerged from bankruptcy rebranded as Exemplar [Source].

New York
All eyes on MetLife Stadium, which hosts the World Cup final on July 19; the city's downtown scene is being championed by Awake NY and young artists like Omi [Source]. U.S. Soccer's "11x11" tapped a designer in each host city to build retail collections [Source].
Los Angeles
L.A.'s 11x11 World Cup collection was designed by Undefeated, drawing on vintage European football scarves [Source]. Revolve Group also launched a new in-house label, Revolve Los Angeles [Source].
Chicago
Hometown hero Joe Freshgoods remains the city's loudest streetwear export, with his "Let's Get Weird" New Balance run; SAIC's MODA show and Newcity's Design 50 spotlighted the emerging local scene [Source][Source].
