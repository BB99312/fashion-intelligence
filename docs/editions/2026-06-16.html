Lavender Linen, a Resale Boom, and a World Cup Streetwear Surge

The state of fashion as of today — what's being worn now, what's predicted next, the releases and emerging designers people are talking about, where consumers are actually spending, and the streetwear brands rising fastest. Every claim is sourced; rumors are labeled.

Executive Summary
  • Summer 2026 is a season of "slow luxury": linen and hemp lead the fabric story, fluid relaxed silhouettes dominate, and Burnished Lilac, butter yellow, and espresso brown are the defining colors.
  • Fall/Winter 2026 predictions point to sharp tailoring and richness: skirt suits, fur and shearling texture, plaid and brocade, with chartreuse, royal purple, and power black leading the palette.
  • Resale is the year's biggest consumer-behavior story: US online resale is projected to reach $31 billion in 2026 (up from $26B in 2025), with secondhand luxury spending up roughly fivefold — value, not just sustainability, is the driver.
  • Emerging designers are breaking through via social before the runway: Aflalo, Jude, Alfie, and Abra are entering celebrity closets; downtown minimalism with a '90s edge is the through-line.
  • The FIFA World Cup has turned streetwear into fashion's loudest stage: Nike and Adidas have mobilized Jacquemus, NOCTA, Willy Chavarria, and G-Dragon, while indie labels like Greedy Unit and Patta ride the wave.
  • Industry pulse: Pitti Uomo 110 opens today in Florence; Pieter Mulier takes the helm at Versace July 1; the 2026 creative-director reshuffle continues to reset the luxury landscape.
01 Current Season Trends Summer 2026 — colors, silhouettes, fabrics & patterns, and the collections shaping them

We are at the heart of Summer 2026, and the season's defining mood is what textile forecasters are calling "slow luxury" — natural fibers, fluid shapes, and a desaturated, sun-bleached palette. Below is the breakdown across the four trend pillars the season is being judged on.

Colors
Burnished Lilac (a smoky, desaturated lavender) has peaked as the color of the summer, joined by butter yellow and an unexpectedly luxe espresso brown in linen and silk. Brights — cobalt blue, cherry red — carry the bolder end of the retail floor, while minty green, Millennial pink, and creamy whites cover the soft neutrals. [Autum Love · Anuprerna]
Silhouettes
Comfort-driven and fluid. Resort-ready linen separates dominate — kimono-style cover-ups, drawstring pants, safari jackets — alongside relaxed tailoring. Fluid movement is the overarching theme of the season; structure is soft, never rigid. [Fabriclore]
Fabrics
Linen is the defining fabric of the season, with hemp and cotton-linen blends close behind, prized for breathability and raw texture. Soft viscose and silk fill the elevated end. European brands in particular have leaned hard into natural-fiber sourcing. [Italian Fashion Sourcing · Fabriclore]
Patterns
Large-scale florals — peonies, wildflowers, abstract roses — lead statement pieces, set against minimal backdrops. Botanical and leaf motifs (palm, cacti, tropical flora) remain strong. The look is romantic but restrained. [All About Fabrics]

Collections shaping the current moment: Dior's Dioriviera (terry-cloth Book Tote, botanical prints) and Alo's Summer Atelier (cashmere, silk, linen, fronted by Candice Swanepoel and Behati Prinsloo) are the resort statements defining premium summer dressing right now. Celine's Automne 2026, merging sharp volumes with edge, is the transitional collection already in stores. [RUSSH]

02 Next Season Predictions Fall/Winter 2026 runway signals — plus an early read on Spring 2027

The Fall/Winter 2026 collections shown in February are now arriving in stores, giving a clear read on autumn. The headline: a swing away from summer's softness toward sharp tailoring, rich texture, and saturated color.

Colors
Chartreuse and royal purple appeared consistently across fashion month via tonal clashing and color blocking. Full-black "power" looks re-emerged, dominating the Milan runways. Brocade-driven jewel tones — burgundy, chocolate — round out occasionwear. [Who What Wear]
Silhouettes
The skirt suit returns in force. Tom Ford paired collarless jackets with pencil skirts; The Row showed checked wool with fur trim; N21 offered peplum jackets with ballooning sleeves. Sharp, body-conscious tailoring is the dominant FW26 shape. [Coveteur]
Fabrics
Texture is the focus. Louise Trotter's Bottega Veneta overflowed with fur, pile, shearling, and fringe. Brocade, jacquard, bouclé, and beaded embellishment carried Dior, Valentino, and Conner Ives — occasionwear is the fastest-growing category for the season. [Coveteur]
Patterns
Plaid, tartan, and checkerboard were among the biggest prints of the season, led by Chloé's check story — accessible enough that fast-fashion interpretations are already landing. Rich brocade and intricate embroidery add dimension at the luxury end. [Who What Wear]

Looking further out — Spring 2027: Pitti Uomo 110 opens today in Florence, launching the SS27 menswear buying season, followed by Milan and Paris. Early expectation is a reaction against the logo-saturated World Cup summer — a return to quiet, refined Italian tailoring and tonal dressing. [FashionUnited]

04 Consumer Behavior Where shoppers are actually spending — the market read

The defining consumer story of 2026 is the structural rise of resale. As primary-market prices climb, shoppers are chasing value, quality, and uniqueness in secondhand — and luxury resale in particular is surging.

+22%

Resale now accounts for over 10% of US apparel, footwear, and accessories dollar sales (January 2026), and the secondhand market is growing two to three times faster than the first-hand market. Notably, secondhand luxury spending has jumped roughly fivefold — buyers are trading down from full-price luxury into pre-owned, while discount apparel grows in parallel. The top stated reasons for buying secondhand are value for money, quality, uniqueness, and convenience, with sustainability now a secondary motivator. [ThredUp / Chain Store Age · eMarketer · Fortune]

On the new-goods floor, spending is concentrated in immediate seasonal need: bold-color summer pieces (cobalt, butter yellow, cherry red), linen separates, denim jorts, and thong/wedge sandals are the top movers at Nordstrom and NET-A-PORTER. The broader picture, per McKinsey's State of Fashion 2026 ("When the rules change"), is a value-conscious, polarized market — growth at the discount and resale ends, pressure in the middle. [Who What Wear · McKinsey]

05 Streetwear Emerging brands, the collaborations driving culture, and the biggest releases

Streetwear's center of gravity this summer is the FIFA World Cup (hosted across the US, Mexico, and Canada), which has become the largest collaboration event in recent memory — Nike and Adidas have each mobilized a dozen-plus designer partnerships, and blokecore (soccer jerseys as everyday wear) is the dominant street look across all three US markets.
